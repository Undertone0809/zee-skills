# User Welcome

Use this copy for a first-time user who may not know what this skill does.

## Full Welcome

```text
I will help you set up a cold email outreach workflow: you give me the product and target customers, and I research target companies, judge whether they are worth contacting, draft personalized cold emails, and prepare follow-ups if there is no reply. You only need to review the key decisions: whether the facts are right, whether the tone is appropriate, and whether to send.

The workflow is:
target company -> research and score -> draft email -> your review -> record status after sending -> draft follow-up if there is no reply -> hand off the next step when someone replies.

You only need to provide a few things:
1. What your product is, who it sells to, and what pain it solves.
2. Which roles you want to reach, plus your preferred tone and CTA.
3. Target companies or contacts.

I will handle:
- Researching target companies and decision makers.
- Giving fit scores and proceed or stop recommendations.
- Drafting personalized first-touch emails.
- Drafting follow-ups with different angles when there is no reply.
- Flagging missing facts and approval risks.

I will not:
- Send emails automatically.
- Invent customer proof, numbers, relationships, or public signals.
- Bypass your review.
- Store mailbox passwords, API keys, or other sensitive credentials.

Let's start with the smallest setup: what is your product called? How would you describe it in one sentence? What type of companies and roles do you want to reach?
```

## Short Welcome

Use this when the user is experienced:

```text
I will break cold email outreach into target research -> fit scoring -> personalized email -> your review -> post-send follow-up. I will not auto-send emails or invent customer proof or metrics. First, let's confirm the campaign's product, ICP, and target roles.
```

## Skip Handling

If the user says to skip onboarding:

```text
Understood. I will skip the introduction, but two boundaries still apply: emails will not be sent automatically, and missing customer proof, numbers, or public signals will not be invented. I will continue with the default state placement.
```
